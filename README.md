Overview: 
This project analyzes retail customer data to understand purchasing behavior, product preferences, and marketing campaign effectiveness. 
Using Excel, SQL, Python, and Tableau, the analysis focuses on identifying high-value customers and improving campaign targeting.

Problem Statement:
Retail businesses often run marketing campaigns without clear targeting, leading to low response rates and inefficient use of resources. 
The challenge is to identify customer segments that are more likely to respond and spend.

Project Objectives:

1. Analyze customer demographics and spending behavior
2. Evaluate campaign performance and response patterns
3. Identify high-value and high-response customer segments
4. Understand product-wise purchase trends
5. Support data-driven marketing decisions

Data Description:
Structured dataset (customer + marketing data).
Includes demographics, income, product spending, and campaign response.
Target variable: Response (campaign acceptance).

Key Insights:

1. Higher-income customers show higher spending, especially on wine
2. Wine and meat products contribute the highest revenue
3. Campaign response rate is low, indicating poor targeting
4. Majority customers belong to graduation-level and married segments
5. Customer behavior varies across income and demographic groups

Outcome:

1. Identified high-value customer segments
2. Highlighted top-performing product categories
3. Evaluated effectiveness of marketing campaigns
4. Built interactive dashboards for business insights

Impact:

1. Enables better customer targeting
2. Improves campaign ROI
3. Supports product-focused marketing strategies
4. Enhances decision-making using data insights

Tools & Technologies Used: Excel, SQL, Python , Tableau
